About

Disputationes Mathematicae is an independent international journal devoted to mathematical scholarship in both pure and applied mathematics. The journal provides an open venue for carefully written research articles, mathematical notes, expository contributions, and works that clarify important ideas, methods, or problems across the mathematical sciences.

The journal is intended for authors and readers who value clarity, rigor, and accessibility. It welcomes submissions from established researchers, early-career mathematicians, independent scholars, and students whose work meets scholarly standards and contributes meaningfully to mathematical discussion.

As an online-only open-access journal, Disputationes Mathematicae is designed to make mathematical knowledge available without subscription barriers, publication charges, or access fees. The journal is operated independently and relies on voluntary editorial work, academic responsibility, and a commitment to fair peer review.

The scope of the journal includes, but is not limited to, algebra, analysis, geometry, topology, number theory, combinatorics, logic, probability, mathematical physics, applied mathematics, and interdisciplinary work with substantial mathematical content.

Mission

The mission of Disputationes Mathematicae is to promote the free exchange of mathematical ideas through accessible, rigorous, and high-quality scholarly publishing.

The journal seeks to provide an international forum for both established and emerging researchers while ensuring that mathematical knowledge remains freely available to all.

Disputationes Mathematicae aims to support work that is mathematically sound, clearly presented, and useful to the wider research community. The journal values precise argumentation, transparent exposition, and constructive editorial communication.

The journal also seeks to encourage intellectual independence. Mathematics develops not only through large research programs, but also through concise observations, new proofs, careful reformulations, open problems, and unexpected connections between fields. Disputationes Mathematicae gives space to these forms of contribution when they are presented with rigor and scholarly care.

By maintaining open access and a no-fee publication model, the journal works to reduce barriers between authors and readers. Its goal is to help mathematical ideas circulate freely, fairly, and permanently.
