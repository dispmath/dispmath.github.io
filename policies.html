Policies

Peer Review Policy

All research submissions are evaluated through a single-blind peer review process.

Each manuscript is first assessed by the editorial team for relevance, scholarly quality, and adherence to the journal's scope. Manuscripts deemed suitable for publication are then reviewed by at least one independent referee with expertise in the relevant field.

Reviewers remain anonymous to authors. Editorial decisions are based on the scholarly merit, originality, clarity, and mathematical soundness of the submitted work.

Submissions authored by members of the Editorial Board are handled independently by another editor to avoid conflicts of interest.

The journal is committed to maintaining fair, constructive, and rigorous evaluation standards.

Open Access Policy

Disputationes Mathematicae is a fully open-access journal.

All articles are freely and permanently available online immediately upon publication.

The journal does not charge:

  • Article Processing Charges (APCs)
  • Submission fees
  • Publication fees
  • Subscription fees
  • Access fees

The journal is supported through voluntary editorial work and independent operation.

Publication Ethics

Authors are expected to submit original work that has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by another publication.

All sources must be properly acknowledged.

Plagiarism, data fabrication, falsification, and other forms of academic misconduct are not tolerated.

Editors and reviewers are expected to treat all submissions confidentially and evaluate manuscripts solely on academic merit.

Copyright Policy

Authors retain copyright of their work.

By publishing in Disputationes Mathematicae, authors grant the journal a non-exclusive right to publish, distribute, and archive the article.

Unless otherwise specified, articles are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License (CC BY 4.0).

Conflict of Interest Policy

Authors, reviewers, and editors should disclose any potential conflicts of interest that may influence the review or publication process.

Editorial decisions must remain independent of personal, financial, institutional, or professional considerations.
